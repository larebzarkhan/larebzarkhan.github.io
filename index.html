About

I grew up in Pakistan, studied electrical engineering at NUST, and spent two years running network operations for PTCL — leading a field team, maintaining 1,200 connections, and learning how telecom infrastructure works at ground level.

An Erasmus Mundus scholarship took me to Aston (UK), Osaka (Japan), and Sant'Anna (Italy) for a joint master's in photonic integrated circuits. That led to a European Industrial Doctorate in applied machine learning for optical networks, split across Pisa, Lisbon, and Lannion.

Now I'm at Meta in London, designing the optical transport network that carries their global traffic. When I'm not at a terminal I travel slowly and read widely.
